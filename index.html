On the hunt for answers to the bigger questions

With a curious mind and a Master's degree in Theoretical Physics from KTH Royal Institute of Technology, I gladly devote myself to studying the mysteries and peculiarites of our Universe. After successfully defending my master thesis on A Machine Learning Approach for Comprehending Cosmic Expansion I worked at the Department of Astronomy at Stockholm University as a Research Assistant/Engineer. I was responsible for developing a new statistical software package in Python for spectral timing analysis, which is a primal tool for analyzing high-energy radiation from objects such as galactic X-ray binaries and black hole candidates. Most recently, in January 2022, I started my Phd in "Machine Learning the initial conditions of the Universe" at the Physics Department at Stockholm University with Jens Jasche as my supervisor. Using statistical methods in a Bayesian forward modeling framework, we're reconstructing the primordial density field, from which the observable Universe evolved through the work of gravity. I'm participating in multi-year research efforts within the the Aquila consortium and the Simons Collaboration on Learning the Universe.
